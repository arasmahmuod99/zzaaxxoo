    • History of Zakho

    History of Zakho In the early days of Islam, the city expanded to the south to the current Al-Hussainiya locality and was called (Hasiniya Al-Khabur). It was mentioned by the Arab poet Abu Al-Ala Al-Ma'ari and the famous Venetian traveler Marco Polo. The Hassaniya Al-Khabur was destroyed by the Turkish Al-Ghaz tribes in 1041 AD and later rebuilt in the central island, which became the nucleus of the city The current. Then it became an independent emirate in the name of the Emirate of Sindia. When the emirate weakened, the Badinan princes were able to annex it to their emirate in 1470 AD. Then the city was under the control of the Ottomans and Zakho became a district in 1864. In the year 1918 the British forces entered the city and practically ended the Ottoman control over it and then the city was annexed The Kingdom of Iraq. Successive rulers who took power in Iraq in the royal and republican eras visited the city because it was an important strategic center for Iraq.

    • General conditions at the beginning of the twentieth century

    At the beginning of the twentieth century, the citizens of Zakho lived a difficult period, dominated by poverty, ignorance and disease as a result of the wrong policy by the Ottoman Empire in the region, in addition to the harsh weather conditions in the winter. Al-Khabour for consecutive years was the most severe in 1903. Despite this severe suffering, the natural phenomena that appeared at the beginning of the twentieth century were ominous omens that caused panic in the hearts of the people. In 1902, red snow fell. In abundance, he generated a bad and frightening impression on the people, believing that blood would flow and cover its red color, as it covered the snow and changed its bright white color to red. It was not a passing event, but rather it was so important and strange that it was taken as a calendar and for many years to be inferred on the events. In 1907, the snow fell profusely, as it lasted for seven days and nights, and that year was called the year (Bafra Mazen). What a big snow year. In 1911, a comet appeared in the sky of the city, which the people considered a bad omen for them. In the year 1914, the First World War broke out, and despite the city's distance from the battlefields, the participation of the Ottoman Empire in that war cast a shadow over the city. One of the most important events that happened to the city during the First World War was the travel of Belk, meaning the travel of death in 1914, as well as the failure of the plan that had been prepared for the attack on the Christians of Zakho in 1915 by the clans of the Shernakh region.     The rise of the Kurdish revolution

    At the beginning of the sixties the relationship worsened a lot between the Prime Minister of Iraq, Abdul Karim Qassem and the Kurdistan Democratic Party. These bad relations between both parties affected the situation in the country and eventually led to the outbreak of an armed conflict between both parties at the beginning of September 1961, when the affiliated militants The Kurdistan Democratic Party seized the Zakho Strait on the night of September 8 and blocked the strait on September 9 without being exposed to passers-by. On the night of 9-10/9 their strength strengthened in various places in the mountains surrounding the city and on the night of 10-11/9 and after midnight the control was achieved On the city on 9/20 the Iraqi forces began attacking Zakho in order to regain control of it. The next day, a brigade of the Iraqi army led by Major General Hassan Abboud, along with large forces of Kurdish militias loyal to the central government, took control of the city after the Kurdish militants withdrew from it. On day 5 3 / From 1962, Issa Siwar was appointed commander of the Zakho region, and the fighting continued between the Kurdish movement led by Mullah Mustafa Barzani and the Iraqi army and the Kurdish militia loyal to him in Zakho throughout those years. One of the most prominent battles that took place between the Kurdish movement and the Iraqi army is the battle of Gali Zakho or Wadi Zakho, which took place on the first of April of the year 1962. After the coup of February 8 of the year 1963 and the fall of the regime of leader Abdul Karim Qasim, the firing between both parties was stopped. The two sides entered into peaceful negotiations, but they failed, and then the fighting resumed in June of the same year. The participation of the Syrian forces in the war operations against the Kurdish movement led to more suffering for the residents of the area. In the month of October of 1963, a violent battle erupted between the movement. The Kurdish forces on the one hand and the Syrian forces in the Qizaf Valley area in the Banstani area in Zakho. In September of 1965, a battle took place between the Iraqi army and the Kurdish militia loyal to it on the one hand and the Kurdish movement on the other hand in the Levi area (Bizah, Bitas, Armasht) located near Zakho city. On June 11 of the same year, a warplane belonging to the Iraqi army fell over the city sky, and the commander of the warplane landed safely in Al-Nasara neighborhood (one of the neighborhoods of Zakho) and its wreckage was scattered on the outskirts of the city, but the real reason for the fall of that war plane was not known.

    • History of Zakho after 1991

    After Iraq annexed Kuwait on August 2, 1990 and the events that followed, the countries of the Western coalition decided to expel the Iraqi forces from Kuwait by force. The Iraqi government threatened Turkey that it would respond to any military attack launched from its territory, and since Turkey was one of the coalition countries Western opposition to the annexation of Kuwait to Iraq, Turkey will respond in return, and the city of Zakho will be the first target city in this confrontation, so the people of Zakho decided to leave the city for fear of a possible war, but the Iraqi authorities at the time prevented the people from leaving at first, and on January 15, 1991 the authorities allowed The people had to evacuate the city. On January 18, at 4:30 am, warplanes of the coalition forces bombed the Iraqi anti-aircraft forces in the city. On the 22nd of the same month, Zakho was empty of residents, and the Iraqi forces mined all the bridges in the city and various areas of the Zakho Strait with large quantities of TNT for fear of any invasion from the Turkish side by the coalition forces, as well as the transfer of some important departments In the city to the city of Dohuk. On Wednesday, January 30, reassurance returned to the people of Zakho, especially after the Iraqi ambassador to Turkey confirmed that the Iraqi government does not intend at all to respond to the coming raids by the coalition forces launched through the territory of Turkey, and in the evening of that day at 10:50 an earthquake struck the city of Zakho For four seconds, but no damage occurred. On February 28, 1991, US President George Bush Sr. declared in a press conference a ceasefire 100 hours after the start of the ground offensive, as the Iraqi forces were expelled from Kuwait. It is worth noting that the city has suffered from a sharp rise in prices throughout the war period, as well as from a total power outage, as well as a fuel cut from the city, as is the case with the rest of the cities of Iraq.
