The best tourist places in Zakho

Dalal Bridge

Dalal Bridge It is one of the most prominent historical monuments in the city. This bridge was erected on the Khabur River, east of Zakho, and there are many rumors about its construction. Hammertain says that this bridge dates back to the time of the Romans, and Askive says it dates back to the era of the Romans, and the Roman commander Seleucus ordered it to be built. A number of Iraqi archaeologists believe that one of the Badinan sultans built this bridge, but it is true that this bridge is one of the oldest monuments in the region and it is possible that one of the Behdinan sultans had maintained and restored it.

Sharanish

It is a village and a tourist resort located in the Zakho district in the Dohuk governorate, with a population of about 570 people, and most of the residents of the village are Chaldean and Assyrian Christians, with the presence of Muslim Kurds, as there were Jews in the past. Near the village there is a waterfall and a resort frequented by tourists. There is also Bhairi Cave, which is 5 km away.
